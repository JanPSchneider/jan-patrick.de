sense-chess

chess redesign

A chess system for
improved gameplay of beginners and
for intelligent analysis and training.

The problem is that chess only involves a certain amount of challenge when players with different skill level play against each other. Either ts is too easy or too hard.
Because in conventional chess games of an advanced against a beginner, the advanced feels under-challenged. And if he shows his skills, this leads to frustration of the beginner.

Also you don't have the possibility to gain information and insights about your gameplay and tactics - the whole training aspect. And you cannot learn chess without any additional instruction.

how to learn chess without anything additional

When playing a game you want to have fun, you want to be good and win. So we decided it is very important to learn the game itself while playing at the physical board.
We focused on new players and their way to learn. Learning by doing, trying and playing over and over again is the common way. So all you have to do to get help is touching a chessman.

When touching it for 15 seconds the board highlights all fields this piece can be moved to while this move. If that is not enough it shows you the best move of this piece, 10 seconds later. If you still touch the piece you then get highlighted the best piece you can move right now and if that isn't enough it shows you the best move by the current best piece.

Looking like the chessboard at home

Very important is the transferability of the game and it's rules from one board to another. That is why the board and the chessmen are looking 'normally' and the technology is hidden and invisible. You only see the power cable but you even don't need to install anything anywhere.

You can focus on learning and playing chess without any distraction.

how to interact

All you have to do to get help is touching a chessman. We figured out many different methods but this seems to be the most intuitive and simple one for almost every type of user. To improve the product we used the morphological box and other methods.

prototyping

We use capacitive sensors (8x8 matrix) at an Arduino sending signals via USB to Processing running on an computer. After being processed this data is sent to our Node.js server, running the whole prototype with our database of all moves and games, the JavaScript libraries (chess AI and chess rules). After this processing and saving the LED field data is sent via Processing to another Arduino, powering the LEDs. With this two independant communications we can ensure that no data is lost and while the whole communication is local there is no problem with loading or waiting time.

We build multiple board and chessmen prototypes and improved everything in an recurrent redesign.

construction

Creating a clean but transferability look was challenging in construction. The board contains all technology and the chessmen are 3D printed conductive but do not contain any additional technology.

chessmen

The transferability of the chessmen is very important. So we designed them with common characteristics but still modern and clean.

You can have a look at the documentation, visit the projects website or view the code and the chessmen design (icons and 3D models) on GitHub.
