I'm an Internet of Things student
at the University of Applied Sciences
in Schwaebisch Gmuend
and intern at IXDS.

Being an IoT student is UX design for the overall experience. It all begins with knowing the user and creating the whole system, like the use of specific technologies (back-end) and ends in product and service design.

Intuitive, simple, functional and clean interfaces are my ambition, while making the whole interaction way easier and more understandable by intelligent use of technology and user centered design, having a critical eye at details and not losing attention on the bigger picture.

In my spare time I'm busy reading about or testing new technologies and prototyping tools, playing the piano, riding my bike or the motorcycle and meeting friends.

tools right now

I am using Adobe Creative tools like XD, Illustrator and InDesign as well as all needed programming languages for an project (mostly web based like JavaScript, sometimes Arduino or C, Java). Prototyping tools like Marvel, noodl and Protopie were limited too soon (except Sketch). When creating scalable designs and prototypes running on almost any device, code is my favourite tool, mostly as an react app but always in use with git.

work

Learning by doing is key of my workflow and I like working that way since a long time. Rethinking using methods like the double diamond, morphological box or just by synthesizing the researched insights, feedback and pain points in an interdisciplinary team is key of my User-centered design work. That's why I use a lot of scribbles, Post-Its and my notebook. Prototyping (paper, MVP or fully functional) is resulting out of this workflow.
