I'm an Internet of Things student
at the University of Applied Sciences
in Schwaebisch Gmuend
and working student at BSH Home Appliances Group.

Being able to enhance people's lives by designing products from the beginning is amazing!

That's why I would say that the best IoT is invisible IoT. If the technic is working that good that it knows what the user wants by itself or even before he knows.

In my free time I'm busy reading about or testing new technological gadgets and prototyping tools, making music or ride my bike to relax and to get a clear head.

tools

I am using Adobe Creative tools like XD, Illustrator and InDesign as well as all needed programming languages for an project (mostly web based like JavaScript, sometimes Arduino). I did use a lot of different tools (noodl, ...) but moostly I reached their limitations too soon.
Trying new frameworks, prototyping tools and materials is important for me, especially because I want my work and myself to be up-to-date.

work

I like working on my computer, but sometimes I prefer notes in my block. Rethinking is the most important part of my work. That's why I use a lot of scribbles, Post-Its and various design methods to stimulate and record my thoughts to get known the user.
But talking to and knowing an user is the best way, thata's why I like personal talks. In my opinion there is no user centered design without prototyping and testing and redesigning. Working within multiple iterations is the best way to go for the best product.
